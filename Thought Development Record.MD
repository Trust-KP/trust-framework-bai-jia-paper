## Purpose

This note records the developmental chain through which the
four-pillar architecture of the KP Conjecture was observed across
domains.

The four dimensions are defined consistently throughout:

| Dimension | Chinese | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Ontology | 釋 | what the system means and what counts within it |
| Structure | 構 | how elements are organised and related |
| Regime | 度 | what is permitted and what constrains the system |
| Realisation | 行 | how the system executes and produces effects |

Within each dimension, four internal primitives apply:

| Primitive | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Interpretation | what exists within this dimension |
| Structure | how it is organised within this dimension |
| Conditions | what is permitted within this dimension |
| Continuation | whether it persists within this dimension |

### Naming Conventions

The framework uses four vocabulary layers:

| Layer | English | Chinese | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dao | Meta Being | 道体 / 道系 / 道制 / 道承 | ground of all systems |
| Meta | Meta State | 元释 / 元构 / 元度 / 元行 | meta-system dimensions |
| Being | Being | 本体 / 体系 / 体制 / 体承 | system existence structure |
| State | State | 釋 / 構 / 度 / 行 | system observational dimensions |

TD-1 through TD-10 and TD-11 use State vocabulary (釋/構/度/行),
as they describe concrete observable systems.

TD-12 through TD-16 use Dao vocabulary
(道体/道系/道制/道承), as they describe ontological
conditions at the Dao layer.

### TD-12: Existence

*Ontological condition: Existence*

| Dimension | Chinese | Expression |
|---|---|---|
| Meta Being | 道体 | Being |
| Meta Structure | 道系 | Structure of Being |
| Meta Regime | 道制 | Conditions of Being |
| Meta Realisation | 道承 | Continuation |

Four axioms follow:

**Axiom 1 — Being.**
An entity exists if and only if it is distinguishable from
non-existence.

**Axiom 2 — Structure.**
An entity exists only insofar as it possesses internal or
relational structure.

**Axiom 3 — Conditions.**
An entity exists only under conditions that permit its structure
to hold.

**Axiom 4 — Continuation.**
An entity exists through time only if its structure is sustained
under its conditions.

### TD-15: Boundary

Boundary is not a system.

It is the transition at which distinction becomes possible.

| Dimension | Chinese | Expression |
|---|---|---|
| Meta Being | 道体 | the onset of distinguishability |
| Meta Structure | 道系 | the minimal separation — this and that |
| Meta Regime | 道制 | the limit at which distinction holds |
| Meta Realisation | 道承 | transition into Possibility |

Boundary does not exist independently. It marks the point where
Indeterminacy gives rise to distinction.

> 未分无界，既分而界生。

### TD-17: Realisation Boundary — The +1 Rule

Each dimension, when pursued vertically to its limit, transitions
into a realisation layer. This transition is denoted +1.

| Dimension | Chinese | Steps | +1 Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Interpretation | 釋 | 7 | Representation — 表征 |

+1 is not termination. It is the transition from system mechanics

### TD-20: Convergence Properties

**Continuation emerges from Regime:**
Regime +1 → Continuation. Continuation is not an Realisation
output. It emerges from the resolution of constraint conditions.

**Realisation resolves to carrier:**
Realisation +1 → Human or carrier. Action resolves to the
entity that performs it.

**Conditional convergence:**

| Path | Convergence point |
|---|---|
| Realisation | Carrier — human |
| Regime | Continuation |
| Structure | Entity |
| Interpretation | Representation |

Systems do not converge to a single primitive. Each dimension
path converges to a distinct primitive.

### TD-21: Path Properties

Multiple valid paths exist. Paths are reversible in principle
but not in practice.

**Cross-pillar integration:**

## Note on the Trust Framework Four Lenses

The Trust Framework operates through four interpretive lenses:
Coordination, Identity, Structure, and Capacity.

These lenses are not a replication of the four pillars.

They are the four ways an observer evaluates an agent's
continuation capacity, each corresponding to one pillar's
observational perspective:

| Lens | Pillar | What the observer evaluates |
|---|---|---|
| Coordination | 釋 | persistence of relational signals |
| Identity | 度 | persistence of institutional validation |
| Structure | 構 | persistence of procedural compliance |
| Capacity | 行 | persistence of demonstrated performance |

The lenses are therefore observer-side evaluation mechanisms,
not system-side structural dimensions. Each lens asks the same
question from a different vantage point:

> Will this agent's contribution to continuation persist?

Identity in particular reflects the output of Regime — the
institutional validation process that produces durable markers
of recognised status. A qualification, a rating, a credential:
these are what Regime produces, and what the Identity lens reads.

## Summary

This developmental record documents the emergence of the
four-pillar architecture through observation across ten primary
domains, one highest system, and five ontological conditions,
followed by fifteen structural properties.

In every case examined, the same four dimensions appear as the
irreducible observational structure of any system.

The KP Conjecture proposes that this is not coincidence but
constraint. The challenge remains open: produce a system that
cannot be described within this framework.
