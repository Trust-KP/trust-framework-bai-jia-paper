## Core Thesis

Existence is not a separate stage but the **bounded identity that emerges when Regime constraints complete the integration of all four pillars within Possibility space.**

When Regime (度) applies constraints to Possibility (superposition), the result is:

- 釋: A singular meaning (interpretation becomes singular, not multiple)
- 構: A coherent structure (possibility space collapses to one organization)
- 度: Defined boundaries (constraints create “this entity” vs “not this entity”)
- 行: Observable manifestation (identity operates as coherent agent)

These four dimensions constitute Existence. **Existence is complete when all four dimensions are present.**

## The Four Dimensions of Existence

### Dimension 1: 釋 (Identity-Meaning)

**What it is:** The singular, recognized identity. “This is THAT.”

**Function:** Makes the entity recognizable as distinct entity

**Examples:**

- Quantum: “Particle has position +5, not superposed”
- Person: “I am [name], not multiple possible selves”
- Organization: “We are [company X], not undefined collective”
- Citizen: “I am [person Y] in records, distinct from all others”
- Law: “This defendant IS guilty (singular judgment)”

**Necessity:** Without singular meaning, entity cannot be recognized. Existence requires identity that observers can label and reference.

-----

### Dimension 2: 構 (Form-Structure)

**What it is:** The coherent organization that persists. “This is made of THAT.”

**Function:** Makes the entity internally coherent and replicable

**Examples:**

- Quantum: “Wave function is definite eigenstate”
- Person: “Body organized as coherent organism”
- Organization: “Company structure (org chart, roles, systems)”
- Citizen: “Documented identity (birth certificate, records, legal status)”
- Law: “Written judgment with reasoning that can be reviewed”

**Necessity:** Without coherent structure, entity would be formless or random. Existence requires organization that can be transmitted and understood.

-----

### Dimension 3: 度 (Boundary-Will)

**What it is:** The constraints and autonomy that define limits. “This is bounded BY THAT.”

**Function:** Makes the entity bounded and agentive

**Examples:**

- Quantum: “Uncertainty principle + definite eigenvalue = this state, not that state”
- Person: “Body boundary + conscious will = this person’s agency”
- Organization: “Legal charter + decision authority = organization’s boundaries”
- Citizen: “Rights + duties = citizen’s constraints and freedoms”
- Law: “Sentencing + appeal limits = judgment’s binding boundary”

**Necessity:** Without boundaries and will, entity would be infinite or passive. Existence requires defined limits and capacity for agency.

-----

### Dimension 4: 行 (Expression-Operation)

**What it is:** The observable manifestation and functioning. “This DOES THAT.”

**Function:** Makes the entity observable and causally effective

**Examples:**

- Quantum: “Particle interacts with apparatus, produces measurable outcomes”
- Person: “Body acts, speaks, moves; has observable effects”
- Organization: “Executes policy, produces services, has measurable output”
- Citizen: “Pays taxes, votes, participates in legal system”
- Law: “Judgment is enforced, changes behavior, has real consequences”

**Necessity:** Without observable operation, entity would be undetectable. Existence requires manifestation in the world.
