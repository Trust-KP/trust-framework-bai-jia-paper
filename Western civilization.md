WESTERN CIVILIZATION EQUIVALENT (STRICT MAPPING, NO RECONSTRUCTION)

------------------------------------------------------------
【0. DEFINITION】
------------------------------------------------------------

Civilisation = a system that:

- generates (Nature / Reality)
- is interpretable (Reason)
- is structured (Knowledge)
- is governed (Institution)
- is executable (Action)
- and sustains continuation (History / Transmission)

------------------------------------------------------------
【1. FOUR-PILLAR STRUCTURE (WESTERN EQUIVALENT)】
------------------------------------------------------------

Western civilisation can be expressed as:

1. Nature (Generative System)
2. Individual (Human System)
3. Institution (Governance System)
4. Knowledge (Knowledge System)

------------------------------------------------------------
【2. SUBSYSTEM FUNCTION DEFINITION】
------------------------------------------------------------

[1] Nature — Generative & Physical System
(Natural Generative System)

Function:
- defines the laws of the world
- provides state space and transformation rules
- governs uncertainty via natural laws

Structure:
- Physics (laws of motion, energy)
- Biology (evolution, adaptation)
- Chemistry (interaction rules)

System role:
“Natural law engine”

------------------------------------------------------------

[2] Individual — Human & Rational System
(Human System)

Function:
- forms expectation (belief, rational anticipation)
- enables decision-making
- builds trust and social relation

Structure:
- Reason (logic, rationality)
- Ethics (good / bad judgement)
- Norms (social behaviour)
- Agency (choice / action)

System role:
“Decision and agency engine”

------------------------------------------------------------

[3] Institution — Governance & Constraint System
(Governance System)

Function:
- defines rules and boundaries
- enforces constraints and incentives
- stabilises coordination

Structure:
- Law (legal systems)
- State (political authority)
- Market (economic rules)
- Organisation (corporate governance)

System role:
“Constraint and order engine”

------------------------------------------------------------

[4] Knowledge — Information & Structural System
(Knowledge System)

Function:
- records history and outcomes
- builds structured understanding
- enables transmission across generations

Structure:
- Science (theory + method)
- History (record)
- Philosophy (interpretation)
- Education (transmission)

System role:
“Information and structure engine”

------------------------------------------------------------
【3. SYSTEM INTERACTION MECHANISM】
------------------------------------------------------------

(1) Nature → Individual
- environment shapes behaviour and expectation

(2) Individual → Institution
- aggregated behaviour creates governance demand

(3) Institution → Individual
- rules constrain and guide action

(4) Individual → Knowledge
- actions produce data and outcomes

(5) Knowledge → Institution
- theory and history refine governance

(6) Knowledge → Individual
- education shapes behaviour and reasoning

(7) Nature → Institution
- environmental change forces institutional adaptation

------------------------------------------------------------
【4. DYNAMIC LOOP (CORE LOOP)】
------------------------------------------------------------

Nature → Individual → Action → Outcome → Knowledge → Institution → Individual → repeat

Equivalent form:

Generation → Decision → Action → Feedback → Adjustment → Continuation

------------------------------------------------------------
【5. FOUR-PILLAR MAPPING (YOUR FRAMEWORK)】
------------------------------------------------------------

释（Interpretation） → Individual + Knowledge
构（Framework）     → Knowledge
度（Regime）        → Institution
行（Operation）     → Individual

生成（Ontology）     → Nature

------------------------------------------------------------
【6. SYSTEM PROPERTIES】
------------------------------------------------------------

1. Completeness:
- covers generation, structure, constraint, execution

2. Interpretability:
- behaviour explained through reason and knowledge

3. Adaptability:
- institutions evolve via knowledge feedback

4. Continuation:
- sustained via record, law, and education

5. Non-determinism:
- uncertainty handled through probability, science, and adaptation

------------------------------------------------------------
【7. FAILURE MODES】
------------------------------------------------------------

Nature failure (environmental breakdown):
- climate, resource collapse

Individual failure (loss of trust / rationality):
- social fragmentation, irrational behaviour

Institution failure (governance breakdown):
- corruption, instability, misaligned incentives

Knowledge failure (loss of structure / truth):
- misinformation, loss of scientific integrity

------------------------------------------------------------
【8. ONE-LINE SUMMARY】
------------------------------------------------------------

Grounded in nature, guided by reason, structured through knowledge, governed by institutions, and sustained through learning, this is the Western expression of the same civilizational system.
